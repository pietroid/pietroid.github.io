From time to time, there are those things that expand your mind to a place you have never imagine it existed. I can say that the book I'm talking about today was responsible and still takes my mind to places I didn't realize before.

![Image of the book]

This is one of the best books I have ever read in my life, and I was never quite able to fully express why. My friends and people around me always found it quite strange because for a couple of reasons: an impossible geometric shape on the book cover, three different german names in the title. The subtitle: "A metaphorical fugue in minds and machines in the spirit of Lewis Carroll". Wait, too much information.

I think that's precisely why I liked this book. I remember discovering it at my first years at college, mere 18-yearish old, my mind was yet full of hope and great ideas, every day being bombarded by new things on math, science, engineering, and boom: I discover this oddly interesting book.
